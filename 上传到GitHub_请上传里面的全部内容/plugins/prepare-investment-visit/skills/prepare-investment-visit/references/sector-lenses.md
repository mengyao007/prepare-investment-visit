# Sector lenses

Load only the relevant lens. Add a second lens for hybrid businesses.

## Enterprise software and AI

Verify ICP, workflow ownership, deployment, ARR and revenue quality, gross margin, retention and expansion, implementation burden, sales cycle, pipeline conversion, inference or cloud cost, security, data rights, model dependency, accuracy evaluation, and human fallback. Distinguish demo quality from production usage.

## Consumer, marketplace, logistics, and local services

Verify active users or assets, cohort retention, frequency, GMV versus revenue, take rate, contribution margin, CAC/LTV, liquidity or route density, utilization, full-cost unit economics, customer and city concentration, service quality, liability, fraud, insurance, and regulatory constraints.

## Robotics and hardware

Verify product generation, BOM, ASP, gross margin, yield, reliability, field failure rate, installation and service cost, certification, supply-chain concentration, production capacity versus output, paid installed base versus shipments, software attach, data feedback, and IP ownership.

## Semiconductors

Verify design architecture, node and foundry, tape-out status, yield, packaging and test, benchmarks under comparable conditions, design wins versus paid mass production, customer qualification cycles, EDA and IP dependencies, inventory, working capital, export controls, and credible path to volume.

## Biotech and medical technology

Verify indication and unmet need, mechanism, preclinical or clinical evidence, endpoints, trial design, safety, regulatory path, CMC and manufacturing, reimbursement, physician workflow, IP term and freedom to operate, licensing obligations, cash runway, and value-inflection milestones. Do not make medical efficacy claims beyond evidence.

## Energy, climate, industrial technology, and new materials

Verify mass and energy balance, TRL, measured versus design performance, feedstock and energy assumptions, uptime and degradation, yield and quality consistency, CAPEX/OPEX boundaries, scale-up factor, permitting and safety, certification, customer qualification, binding offtake, project financing, lifecycle analysis, and ownership of process IP. Treat announced capacity separately from commissioned and stable capacity.

## Manufacturing and supply chain

Verify bookings, orders, revenue recognition, capacity, utilization, yield, lead time, customer concentration, inventory and receivables, warranty, supplier concentration, capex, labor content, make-versus-buy boundaries, and sensitivity to commodity or foreign-exchange movements.

## Fintech and regulated financial services

Verify license and regulated entity, product flow, source and use of funds, risk ownership, approval and loss rates, fraud, unit economics after funding and credit costs, customer acquisition, compliance, data permissions, partner concentration, capital requirements, and regulatory change exposure.

## Cross-sector technical sanity check

Translate the product into an input-process-output diagram. Identify the physical, economic, regulatory, or data conservation rule that cannot be bypassed. Ask for the smallest set of measured values that determines whether the claimed advantage is real.
