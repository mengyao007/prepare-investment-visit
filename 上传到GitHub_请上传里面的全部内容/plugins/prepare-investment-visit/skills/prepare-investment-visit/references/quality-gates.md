# Quality gates

Do not deliver until the applicable checks pass.

## Identity and freshness

- The exact company and relevant legal entities are resolved or ambiguity is disclosed.
- The founder or meeting person's identity is corroborated; same-name risks are disclosed.
- The research cutoff date appears near the beginning.
- Recent developments were searched separately.

## Evidence

- Decision-relevant numbers, customers, roles, financing events, and recent events have nearby citations.
- BP and teaser statements are labeled as company claims unless independently verified.
- Source grades and limitations are included.
- Calculations show assumptions and units.
- `未找到公开披露` is not rewritten as proof that something does not exist.

## Investment usefulness

- The note distinguishes a good market, a good company, strong evidence, and an investable opportunity.
- Competitors include substitutes, incumbents, and potential vertical integration, not only similar startups.
- Questions are linked to hypotheses, contradictions, or hard gates.
- The data-request list is prioritized and proportional to the meeting stage.
- At least one plausible disconfirming or bear-case path is considered.

## Internal consistency

- Dates, units, currencies, and metric definitions are consistent.
- Contract, deployment, active usage, revenue, and cash collection are not conflated.
- Registered capital, financing amount, valuation, and ownership are not conflated.
- Facts, claims, estimates, and inferences remain visibly distinct.

## Confidentiality and tone

- Confidential supplied materials are not quoted beyond what is necessary.
- The language is direct and neutral, without promotional adjectives unsupported by evidence.
- The note states its evidence boundary and does not claim to be formal due diligence.
